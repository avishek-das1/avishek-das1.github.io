Mechano-Chemical Information Processing

Statistical Physics of Computing with Soft Matter

What makes a collection of chemical reactions into a functioning living organism?
How do biological cells perceive and manipulate their noisy world?
How does chemistry and mechanics come together to create intelligent spatiotemporal patterns?

These questions are at the heart of the research in our group. Information processing in soft and living matter often involves a combination of chemical reactions, mechanical forces, and diffusion, without a clear separation between their timescales. We quantify the dynamics of information processing in such systems through novel theories and computational approaches centered in statistical physics and information theory. Our goal is to understand the fundamental design prinicples of computation with mechano-chemical motifs in both biological and synthetic systems.
