Mechano-Chemical Information Processing

Statistical Physics of Computing with Soft Matter

What makes a collection of chemical reactions into a functioning living organism?

How do biological cells perceive and manipulate their noisy world?

How does chemistry and mechanics come together to create intelligent spatiotemporal patterns?

These questions are at the heart of the research in our group. Information processing in soft and living matter often involves a combination of chemical reactions, mechanical forces, and diffusion, without a clear separation between their timescales. We quantify the dynamics of information processing in such systems through novel theories and computational approaches centered in statistical physics and information theory. Our goal is to understand the fundamental design prinicples of mechano-chemical computation in biological and synthetic systems.

Condensate formation under chemical signals

How do rare events affect information processing? We investigate how the nucleation and phase-separation of liquid condensates is used by cells to process chemical signals.

Actuation of motile bacteria

How does sensing and actuation jointly give rise to function? We explore how motile bacteria convert chemical signals into mechanical actuation in order to swim.

Collective rearrangements of cells

Can the information from biochemical signaling give rise to spatial patterns? We uncover how cells rearrange in tissues through mechano-chemical coupling.

Information theory of soft matter

Is there a systematic framework to apply information theory to soft matter? We develop groundbreaking theoretical and computational tools to quantify dynamical information processing.
